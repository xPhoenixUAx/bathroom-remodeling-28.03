2. What Are Cookies?

Cookies are small text files placed on your device when you visit a website. They help websites function properly and provide insights into how users interact with content.

Cookies may include:

  • Session cookies - temporary and removed when you close your browser
  • Persistent cookies - remain on your device until they expire or are deleted
  • First-party cookies - set by this website
  • Third-party cookies - set by external services or providers

3. Types of Cookies We Use

3.1 Essential Cookies

These cookies are necessary for the website to function correctly and cannot be disabled.

They support:

  • Remembering cookie consent preferences
  • Maintaining session stability
  • Ensuring security and accessibility

3.2 Analytics Cookies

These cookies help us understand how visitors interact with the website, allowing improvements to usability, structure, and content clarity.

They may collect anonymized data such as:

  • Page views and navigation patterns
  • Time spent on pages
  • General interaction behavior

We may use tools such as Google Analytics for these purposes.

3.3 Functional Cookies

These cookies support enhanced usability and personalization.

Examples include:

  • Remembering user preferences (such as language or region)
  • Temporarily storing form inputs
  • Supporting smoother interaction with informational tools

3.4 Marketing Cookies

These cookies help measure the effectiveness of outreach and present more relevant content.

They may be used to:

  • Track engagement across pages
  • Improve content relevance
  • Support advertising and remarketing efforts

4. Third-Party Cookies

We may work with third-party services that place cookies on your device, including analytics providers, advertising platforms, or embedded tools.

These third parties may collect information about your activity across different websites. BathScope Guide does not control these cookies, and you should review their respective privacy policies.

5. How to Manage Cookies

You have the ability to accept or decline cookies.

Most browsers accept cookies automatically, but settings can be adjusted to control or block them.

5.1 Browser Controls

You can manage cookies through your browser settings. Instructions are available for common browsers, including:

  • Google Chrome
  • Mozilla Firefox
  • Safari
  • Microsoft Edge

5.2 Opt-Out Tools

You may opt out of certain third-party tracking through tools such as:

  • Google Analytics Opt-Out
  • Digital Advertising Alliance (DAA)
  • Network Advertising Initiative (NAI)

Note: Disabling cookies may affect how certain parts of the website function and limit access to some features.

6. Do Not Track Signals

Some browsers include a "Do Not Track" (DNT) feature.

Currently, there is no consistent standard for interpreting these signals, and this website does not respond to DNT settings.
